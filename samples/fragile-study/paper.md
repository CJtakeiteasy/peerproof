# The Lighthouse Study

## Abstract

We evaluated whether exposure was associated with a continuous outcome in a
small observational sample. The analysis used ordinary least squares regression.

## Methods

Ten observations were analyzed. Outcome was regressed on exposure with an
intercept. The primary claim was evaluated using the estimated exposure
coefficient and its two-sided Student t-test. Statistical significance was
defined as p < 0.05.

## Results

Exposure was positively associated with outcome (β = 1.276, SE = 0.251,
p < 0.001; Figure 1). We therefore conclude that higher exposure is associated
with a higher outcome in this sample.
